About the Challenge

This challenge was born out of the powerful union of three things: love of family, love of mischief, and a love of competition. Originally it was called the Papa Gino's Challenge, after a popular pizza chain in Massachusetts where the founding members were eating on they day that the challenge was formulated.

"It all began at Papa Gino's, hence the very not healthy challenge title. A few of us were eating pizza and ironically discussing how we need more motivation to be healthy. We thought creating a competition would be a fun way to stay on track with nutrition and working out while getting other family members motivated to do the same. After that, a spreadsheet was created and the contest began! It morphed over time to accommodate the goals of the participants."

- Bridget Kelly, Founding Member

"Matt [wanted] to use my dad's competitiveness to make him work out... It worked really well because we are weirdly competitive... My mom [would be] walking around the house like a crazy person at midnight to get more steps."

- Joe Wyman Original Member

“It keeps me motivated to stay healthy.”

- Anne Marie Wyman, Original Member

Point System Guidlines

For each month your goal should be to get 50 points each day. There are four categories for earning points: workouts, steps, food and water, & sleep. Some days this might mean working out a little bit more, some days this might mean focusing on your water intake and eating right. Papa's Challenge was designed to keep you accountable while also being realistic and sustainable.

Workout Points

You can get points for the following activities:

Stretching:

15 minutes of continuous stretching will earn you 5 points, and 30 min of stretching will earn you 15 points

If you do 50 push-ups, squats, or lunges:

You earn 5 points for each set (so 50 pushups, 50 squats, and 50 lunges done in one day will earn you 15 points, as will doing 150 squats in one day).

If you do 25 burpees, pullups, or dips:

You earn 5 points for each set, just like above.

If you do 100 jumping jacks:

You earn 5 points for each set.

If you do a plank for 150 seconds (or 2.5 minutes):

You earn 5 points.

If you do a 10-15 minute core or hip workout:

You earn 10 points for each workout (so 20 if you do both core and hip workout in one day)

If you attend a work out class, go to the gym, or go for a run:

  • Casual movement, light workout- 5 points
  • Starting to sweat, 30 min- 10 points
  • Medium, sweating, pretty challenging 30 min- 15 points
  • Medium to hard, sweating, pretty challenging for 60 min- 25 points
  • Hard, intense sweat, (you arent walking anywhere tomorrow) lasted 60min- 35 points

Steps

For the first 10,000 steps you get (within one day) you earn 10 points. You earn 5 points for every 5, 000 steps after the first 10,000. Also 50 flights of stairs earns you 5 points.

Food and Water

  • Veggies! You earn 10 points for every 3 servings of veggies you eat.
  • Fruit! You earn 2 points for each fruit serving you eat.
  • Clean Eating! Be your own judge, but this should include no soda, chips, cookies, sweets, alcohol.. cereal.. generally known food that ISN'T good for you. The harder you are on yourself the better you'll be. 10 points if you eat Clean!
  • Water! 10 points for 100 oz. OR 1/2 your body weight in oz. This option is capped at 10 point (don't overdo it, you need to find your hydration sweet spot).

Sleep

If you get at least 7 hours of sleep you earn 10 points!

Bonus Points

If you earn 50 points for 3 days in a row, then you get 10 bonus points on the third day. If you earn 50 points for 5 days in a row, then you get 10 bonus points on the fifth day. If you earn 50 points for 6 out of 7 days then you get an additional 10 points! Amazing!
